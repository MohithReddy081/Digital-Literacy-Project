# Case Study: Phishing

**Cybercrime Type:** Phishing

**Overview:**  
Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers attempt to trick individuals into revealing sensitive personal information, such as login credentials, banking details, or credit card numbers. These attacks often come in the form of emails, text messages, or social media messages that appear to be from legitimate organizations.

**How it Happens (Step-by-Step):**  
1. The attacker creates a fake email or website that closely resembles a trusted entity, such as a bank or an online service.  
2. The victim receives a message urging immediate action, often claiming there is a problem with their account.  
3. The victim clicks the provided link, which redirects to the fraudulent website.  
4. The victim enters personal information, believing the site is genuine.  
5. The attacker captures the information and can use it to access accounts, steal money, or commit identity fraud.

**Typical Targets:**  
Anyone with online accounts can be a target, but phishing frequently targets:  
- Bank account holders  
- Students and young adults active on email or social media  
- Employees of organizations for corporate credential theft  

**Consequences:**  
Victims of phishing may face financial loss, unauthorized transactions, identity theft, and long-term damage to credit scores. Organizations may suffer reputational damage, legal liabilities, and loss of sensitive corporate data.  

**Example Incident:**  
In 2020, several users of a major Indian bank received phishing emails mimicking official bank communication. Some victims unknowingly shared OTPs and credentials, resulting in direct financial losses (Source: [The Times of India, 2020](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/)).

**Prevention Tips:**  
- Verify sender email addresses  
- Avoid clicking suspicious links  
- Enable two-factor authentication  
- Keep software updated
