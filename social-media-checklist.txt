1.Social Media Do’s and Don’ts for College Students

Do’s
- Do think before you post to ensure your content is respectful and appropriate.
- Do maintain privacy settings to control who sees your posts.
- Do fact-check information before sharing to avoid spreading misinformation.
- Do engage positively and respectfully with others online.
- Do use social media to build professional networks and learning communities.

Don’ts
- Don’t share personal information like your address or phone number publicly.
- Don’t post anything that could harm your reputation or that of others.
- Don’t engage in online arguments or harassment.
- Don’t click on suspicious links or download unknown files.
- Don’t share copyrighted material without permission.

2. Report Section: Poor Digital Communication Scenario
Poor digital communication can lead to misunderstandings and conflicts, especially in academic or professional settings. For example, a student once sent a sarcastic email to a professor questioning a grading decision, which was interpreted as disrespectful and unprofessional. This resulted in strained communication and affected the student’s relationship with the professor. If the student had taken time to draft a polite and clear email, asking for clarification respectfully, the issue could have been resolved amicably. Clear, thoughtful digital communication—free of sarcasm or ambiguous tone—helps avoid unnecessary problems and maintains positive relationships in both academic and social environments.
